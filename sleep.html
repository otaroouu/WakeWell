Sleep Stages Overview

Human sleep is traditionally divided into multiple stages, each serving a critical biological and neurological function. WakeWell monitors these stages to ensure healthy sleep continuity and timely awakening.

Non-REM Sleep

Non-Rapid Eye Movement (NREM) sleep consists of progressively deeper stages of rest. During this period, brain activity slows, muscle tension decreases, and external awareness is reduced.

These stages are considered stable and self-limiting under normal conditions.

REM Sleep

Rapid Eye Movement (REM) sleep is associated with vivid dreaming, increased brain activity, and temporary muscle paralysis.

REM stages are expected to occur cyclically and resolve without conscious intervention.

Observational Notes

Recent internal observations indicate that extended REM exposure may result in heightened environmental continuity within dream states.

In isolated cases, subjects have reported difficulty distinguishing between dream environments and waking memory.

Advisory Notice

If you experience recurring locations, repeated interactions, or prolonged awareness during sleep, WakeWell recommends maintaining a consistent waking routine.

Avoid intentional sleep deepening techniques unless supervised by a qualified specialist.
